Profile of Cast Away

The mood of Cast Away is gloomy, contemplative, and captivating. The plot centers around isolation, the human spirit, and a "fish out of water". It is a drama and adventure movie. Stylistically, Cast Away has a Hollywood tone. In approach, it is serious and realistic. The pacing is slow. Cast Away takes place, at least partly, on an island. It happens in the 1990s. The movie is known for being a modern classic, a blockbuster, and critically acclaimed. Cast Away is especially suggested for a date night and a family outing.

Summary

Tom Hanks is Chuck Noland, a man in a hurry. His job for Federal Express has him traveling the world on a moment's notice, exhorting the company's employees to speed things up--"never turn your back on the clock." When he's suddenly called away for business on Christmas night, his tolerant longtime girlfriend Kelly (Helen Hunt) drives him to the airport. They have their Christmas in the car--and Chuck plunks an engagement ring into her lap right before he gets on the plane, telling her, "I'll be right back." But an unexpected storm cuts the plane's crew off from radio contact and blows them off course. Chuck is the sole survivor of the resulting crash, and washes up on a completely deserted island. Stranded there, he must give up everything that he once took for granted and learn how to survive all alone in the wilderness. From director Robert Zemeckis, Cast Away is a story of adventure and discovery surrounding one man's will to stay alive. less

Tom Hanks delivered a fine performance in this flick. I could feel every emotion, every phase his character went through reflected in myself as the film progressed. Loneliness as passion, the palpable yearning for human contact, the blurring of the ingrained illusion of time. The cinematography was impressive, both the island and the ocean emerged as the biggest supporting stars of the film. A triumph of scenery as character. Another fine example of landscape in a starring role can be seen in Lawrence of Arabia. It's rare, these days, for a film to be made without the obligatory soundtrack. Hollywood is in love with mood music, sickeningly so at times. Castaway is gloriously free of this curse except for one small indulgence as Hank's character sails out of sight of the island.
